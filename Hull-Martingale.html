We create a Portfolio :
\( \Omega =\! \left[\sigma_2f_2 \right] f_1 - \left[\sigma_1f_1 \right] f_2 \)

Return from Portfolio :
\( \Delta\Omega =\! \left[\sigma_2f_2 \right] \left(\Delta f_1\right) - \left[\sigma_1f_1 \right] \left(\Delta f_2\right) \)
\( = \left[\sigma_2f_2 \right] \left( \mu_1 f_1 \Delta t + \sigma_1 f_1 \Delta W_t \right) -\! \left[\sigma_1f_1 \right] \left( \mu_2 f_2 \Delta t + \sigma_2 f_2 \Delta W_t \right) \)
\( = \left( \mu_1 \sigma_2 f_1 f_2 - \mu_2 \sigma_1 f_1 f_2 \right)\left( \Delta t \right) \)
\( = r \Omega \left( \Delta t \right) \)

So We have :
\( \left( \mu_1 \sigma_2 f_1 f_2 - \mu_2 \sigma_1 f_1 f_2 \right) = \left[\sigma_2f_2 \right] rf_1 - \left[\sigma_1f_1 \right] rf_2 \)

In above Equation \( \left( f_1f_2 \right) \) will cancel out. So We have : \( \left[ \frac{\mu_1 - r}{\sigma_1} \right] =\! \left[ \frac{\mu_2 - r}{\sigma_2} \right] =\! \lambda \)

This \( \lambda \) is called MARKET PRICE OF RISK for the Underlying. We see that, all Derivatives of \( S \) will have SAME Market Price, dependent on the Underlying. So the Excess Return that a typical Derivative of Underlying will have is \( \left( \sigma_i\lambda \right) \). So if this MARKET PRICE OF RISK is Zero then, Derivative's growth rate will be \( r \)
We now assume that\( \left[ \frac{\mu_1 - r}{\sigma_1} \right] =\! \left[ \frac{\mu_2 - r}{\sigma_2} \right] =\! \sigma_2 \). This WORLD is called FORWARD RISK NEUTRAL w.r.t. 2nd Derivative.

So We have :
\( \left[\frac{df_1}{f_1}\!=\! \left(r + \sigma_1 \sigma_2\right) dt + \sigma_1 dW_t \right] \), \( \left[\frac{df_2}{f_2}\!=\! \left(r + \sigma_2 \sigma_2\right) dt + \sigma_2 dW_t \right] \)

Using Ito's Lemma :
\( \left[ d \left(\text{log} f_1\right) \!=\! \left(r + \sigma_1 \sigma_2 - 0.5\sigma_1^2 \right) dt + \sigma_1 dW_t \right] \)
\( \left[ d \left(\text{log} f_2\right) \!=\! \left(r + \sigma_2 \sigma_2 - 0.5\sigma_2^2 \right) dt + \sigma_2 dW_t \right] \)

This gives :
\( \left[ d \left(\text{log} \frac{f_1}{f_2} \right) \!=\! \left(\sigma_1 \sigma_2 - 0.5\sigma_1^2 - 0.5\sigma_2^2 \right) dt + \left( \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 \right) dW_t \right] \)
\( \!=\! -0.5 \left( \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 \right)^2 dt + \left( \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 \right) dW_t \)

This means that that the Ratio \( \left[\frac{f_1}{f_2} \right] \) is basically a Martingale, i.e. it has NO DRIFT term !!!

Valuation - CAP - An IR Option
A CAP has a number of Reset Dates starting from \( T_0 \). In each reset date, the LIBOR is observed. If LIBOR is more than the Strike Rate then Payoff is calculated based on the Difference multiplied with Principal. Payoff will be given in next reset date. Otherwise, NO Payoff. ALSO, on the 1st Payment date, NO Payoff.

So Payoff for a given reset date is - \( \left[ \frac{L \delta_k \max \left[R_k - R_K, \: 0 \right] }{ 1 + \delta_k r_k } \right] \).

Valuation is done using Black's Formula : \( c =\!\! L \delta_k P\left( 0,t_{k+1}\right) \left( F_{0, \:t_k,\:t_{k+\!1}} N \left( d_1 \right) - R_K N \left( d_2 \right) \right) \). ALSO, the Underlying Volatility need to be multiplied with \( \left( t_k \right) ^{0.5} \) because, Our Volatility time-horizon is \( \left( t_k \right) \).

Adjustment - Convexity
HOW to price an Instrument which pays w.r.t. Forward Bond Yield? ANS : \( P \left(0,T \right) E^*\left[ y_T \right] \).

In calculation, We generally replace \( E^*\left[ y_T \right] \) with the Forward Bond Yield i.e. \( y_F \), which to be derived from the Forward Bond Price \( F_B \) (as per the Forward Bond Yield definition).

However the relation between \( F_B \) & \( y_F \) is NOT-Linear i.e. \( F_B = G \left( y_F \right) \). Like, for ZC Bond, We have \( \left[ G \left( y_F \right) =\! \frac{1}{1+y_F \tau} \right] \).

In the Convexity Adjustment, We will use : \( E^*\left[ y_T \right] = y_F + A \). \( A \) is the required Adjustment based on \( G \).
Adjustment - Time
HOW to price an Instrument which pays w.r.t. some Variable in future BUT Payoff will happen Later? ANS : \( P \left(0,T_1 \right) E^*\left[ V_T \right] \).

From above Payoff formula, it is evident that : The CORRECT Expectation will be \( E^{**}\left[ V_T \right] \). So, We need to move from the World which is Forward Risk Neutral w.r.t \( P \left(0,T \right) \) to \( P \left(0,T_1 \right) \). Adjustment - Quantos
Consider the FX for Domestic country \( Y \) as \( S_{Y \to X} \). So, it's process will be \( dS_{Y \to X} = \left( r_Y - r_X \right) S_{Y \to X} dt + \sigma S_{Y \to X} dW_t \)

So, from Ito's Lemma, We have : \( d \left( \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right) = \left( r_X - r_Y + \sigma^2 \right) \left( \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right) dt + \sigma \left( \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right) dW_t \)

Above process is Risk-Neutral process w.r.t. country \( Y \). Means, it is Forward Risk Neutral w.r.t. Money Market for country \( Y \). Idially, We want to move to Forward Risk Neutral w.r.t. Money Market for country \( X \). So We use, the Numeraire Ratio as \( \left[ W =\! \frac{g_X}{g_Y} S_{Y \to X} \right] \), \( \{ \!g \} \) is the Money-Market Account. So Drift in above equation will increase by \( \left( \rho_{W,V} \sigma_V \sigma_W \right), V =\! \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \). BUT, We have \( \rho_{W,V} =\! -\!1, \sigma_W =\! \sigma \). Hence the change in Drift will be just \( -\! \sigma^2 \).

Hence Final process will be \( d \left( \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right) = \left( r_X - r_Y \right) \left( \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right) dt + \sigma \left( \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right) dW_t \)
