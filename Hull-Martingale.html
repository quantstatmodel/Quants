\( \left[\frac{dS}{S}\!=\! \mu dt + \sigma dW_t \right] \)
SPOT
\( \left[\frac{df_1}{f_1}\!=\! \mu_1 dt + \sigma_1 dW_t \right] \)
Derivative 1
\( \left[\frac{df_2}{f_2}\!=\! \mu_2 dt + \sigma_2 dW_t \right] \)
Derivative 2
We create a Portfolio :
\( \Omega =\! \big[\sigma_2f_2 \big] f_1 - \big[\sigma_1f_1 \big] f_2 \)

Return from Portfolio :
\( \Delta\Omega =\! \big[\sigma_2f_2 \big] \big(\Delta f_1\big) - \big[\sigma_1f_1 \big] \big(\Delta f_2\big) \)
\( = \big[\sigma_2f_2 \big] \big( \mu_1 f_1 \Delta t + \sigma_1 f_1 \Delta W_t \big) -\! \big[\sigma_1f_1 \big] \big( \mu_2 f_2 \Delta t + \sigma_2 f_2 \Delta W_t \big) \)
\( = \big( \mu_1 \sigma_2 f_1 f_2 - \mu_2 \sigma_1 f_1 f_2 \big)\big( \Delta t \big) \)
\( = r \Omega \big( \Delta t \big) \)

So We have :
\( \big( \mu_1 \sigma_2 f_1 f_2 - \mu_2 \sigma_1 f_1 f_2 \big) = \big[\sigma_2f_2 \big] rf_1 - \big[\sigma_1f_1 \big] rf_2 \)

In above Equation \( \big( f_1f_2 \big) \) will cancel out. So We have : \( \left[ \frac{\mu_1 - r}{\sigma_1} \right] =\! \left[ \frac{\mu_2 - r}{\sigma_2} \right] =\! \lambda \)

This \( \lambda \) is called MARKET PRICE OF RISK for the Underlying. We see that, all Derivatives of \( S \) will have SAME Market Price, dependent on the Underlying. So the Excess Return that a typical Derivative of Underlying will have is \( \big( \sigma_i\lambda \big) \). So if this MARKET PRICE OF RISK is Zero then, Derivative's growth rate will be \( r \).
We now assume that\( \left[ \frac{\mu_1 - r}{\sigma_1} \right] =\! \left[ \frac{\mu_2 - r}{\sigma_2} \right] =\! \sigma_2 \). This WORLD is called FORWARD RISK NEUTRAL w.r.t. 2nd Derivative.

So We have :
\( \left[\frac{df_1}{f_1}\!=\! \big(r + \sigma_1 \sigma_2\big) dt + \sigma_1 dW_t \right] \), \( \left[\frac{df_2}{f_2}\!=\! \big(r + \sigma_2 \sigma_2\big) dt + \sigma_2 dW_t \right] \)

Using Ito's Lemma :
\( \left[ d \big(\mathbb{LOG} f_1\big) \!=\! \left(r + \sigma_1 \sigma_2 - 0.5\sigma_1^2 \right) dt + \sigma_1 dW_t \right] \)
\( \left[ d \big(\mathbb{LOG} f_2\big) \!=\! \left(r + \sigma_2 \sigma_2 - 0.5\sigma_2^2 \right) dt + \sigma_2 dW_t \right] \)

This gives :
\( \left[ d \left(\mathbb{LOG} \frac{f_1}{f_2} \right) \!=\! \left(\sigma_1 \sigma_2 - 0.5\sigma_1^2 - 0.5\sigma_2^2 \right) dt + \big( \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 \big) dW_t \right] \)
      \( \!=\! -0.5 \big( \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 \big)^2 dt + \big( \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 \big) dW_t \)

This means that that the Ratio \( \left[\frac{f_1}{f_2} \right] \) is basically a Martingale, i.e. it has NO DRIFT term !!!

So Drift in above equation will increase by \( \left[ \big( \rho_{W,V} \sigma_V \sigma_W \big), V =\! \left[\frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right] \right]\). BUT, We have \(\big[ \rho_{W,V} =\! -\!1, \sigma_W =\! \sigma \big] \). Hence the change in Drift will be just \( \big[ \!-\!\!\! \sigma^2 \big] \).

Hence Final process will be \( d \left( \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right) = \big( r_X - r_Y \big) \left( \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right) dt + \sigma \left( \frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}} \right) dW_t \)
