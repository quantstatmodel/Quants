We create a Portfolio :
\( \Omega =\! \big[\sigma_2f_2 \big] f_1 - \big[\sigma_1f_1 \big] f_2 \)

Return from Portfolio :
\( \Delta\Omega =\! \big[\sigma_2f_2 \big] \big(\Delta f_1\big) - \big[\sigma_1f_1 \big] \big(\Delta f_2\big) \)
         \( = \big[\sigma_2f_2 \big] \big( \mu_1 f_1 \Delta t + \sigma_1 f_1 \Delta W_t \big) -\! \big[\sigma_1f_1 \big] \big( \mu_2 f_2 \Delta t + \sigma_2 f_2 \Delta W_t \big) \)
         \( = \big( \mu_1 \sigma_2 f_1 f_2 - \mu_2 \sigma_1 f_1 f_2 \big)\big( \Delta t \big) \)
         \( = r \Omega \big( \Delta t \big) \)

So We have :
         \( \big( \mu_1 \sigma_2 f_1 f_2 - \mu_2 \sigma_1 f_1 f_2 \big) = \big[\sigma_2f_2 \big] rf_1 - \big[\sigma_1f_1 \big] rf_2 \)

In above Equation \( \big[ f_1f_2 \big] \) will cancel out. So We have : \( \left[ {\displaystyle \frac{\mu_1 - r}{\sigma_1} } \right] =\! \left[ {\displaystyle\frac{\mu_2 - r}{\sigma_2}} \right] =\! \lambda \)

This \( \big[ \lambda \big] \) is called MARKET PRICE OF RISK for the Underlying. We see that, all Derivatives of \( \big[ S \big] \) will have SAME Market Price, dependent on the Underlying. So the Excess Return that a typical Derivative of Underlying will have is \( \big[ \sigma_i\lambda \big] \). So if this \( \big[ \mathbb{RISK}_{\mathbb{MARKET\:PRICE}} = \! 0 \big] \) then, Derivative's growth rate will be \( \big[ r \big] \).
We now \( \mathbb{ASSUME}\) that\( \left[ \left[ {\displaystyle\frac{\mu_1 - r}{\sigma_1}} \right] =\! \left[ {\displaystyle\frac{\mu_2 - r}{\sigma_2}} \right] =\! \sigma_2 \right] \).

This World is called FORWARD RISK NEUTRAL w.r.t. 2nd Derivative.

So We have :
\( \left[{\displaystyle\frac{df_1}{f_1}}\!=\! \big(r + \sigma_1 \sigma_2\big) dt + \sigma_1 dW_t \right] \), \( \left[{\displaystyle\frac{df_2}{f_2}}\!=\! \big(r + \sigma_2 \sigma_2\big) dt + \sigma_2 dW_t \right] \)

Using Ito's Lemma :
\( \left[ d \big(\mathbb{LOG} f_1\big) \!=\! \left(r + \sigma_1 \sigma_2 - 0.5\sigma_1^2 \right) dt + \sigma_1 dW_t \right] \)
\( \left[ d \big(\mathbb{LOG} f_2\big) \!=\! \left(r + \sigma_2 \sigma_2 - 0.5\sigma_2^2 \right) dt + \sigma_2 dW_t \right] \)

This gives :
\( \left[ d \left(\mathbb{LOG} {\displaystyle\frac{f_1}{f_2}} \right) \!=\! \left(\sigma_1 \sigma_2 - 0.5\sigma_1^2 - 0.5\sigma_2^2 \right) dt + \big( \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 \big) dW_t \right] \)
                              \( \!=\! -\!\!0.5 \big( \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 \big)^2 dt + \big( \sigma_1 - \sigma_2 \big) dW_t \)

This means that that the Ratio \( \left[{\displaystyle\frac{f_1}{f_2}} \right] \) is basically a \( \mathbb{MARTINGALE}\) i.e. \( \big[ \mathbb{DRIFT} =\! 0\big]\).

In calculation, We generally replace \( \mathbb{E}^*\big[ y_T \big] \) with \( \big[y_F\big] \), which to be derived from \( \big[F_{\mathbb{BOND}}\big] \), according to the Defn.

Relation between \( \big[F_{\mathbb{BOND}}\big] \) & \( \big[y_F\big] \) is NON-Linear i.e. \( \big[F_{\mathbb{BOND}} = G \big( y_F \big)\big] \).
     Like, for \(\mathbb{ZCB}\), We have \( \left[ G \big( y_F \big) =\! {\displaystyle\frac{1}{1+y_F \tau}} \right] \).

Hence, \( \big[ \mathbb{E}^*\big[ y_T \big] = y_F + A \big] \). \( \big[A\big] \) is the required Adjustment based on \( \big[G\big] \).
Adjustment - Time
\(\mathbb{ASSUME}\) : Instrument pays w.r.t. some Variable in future BUT Payoff will happen Later. HOW to Price ??? ANS : \( P \big(0,T_1 \big) \mathbb{E}^*\big[ V_T \big] \).

From above Payoff formula, it is evident that : The CORRECT \(\mathbb{EXPECTATION}\) will be \( \mathbb{E}^{**}\big[ V_T \big] \). So, We need to move from the World which is Forward Risk Neutral w.r.t. \( P \big(0,T \big) \) to \( P \big(0,T_1 \big) \).
So, We define the Numeraire Ratio \( \left[ W = \! {\displaystyle\frac{P \big(0,T_1 \big)}{P \big(0,T \big)}} \right] =\! \left[ {\displaystyle\frac{1}{1\:+\:R \big(0,T, T_1 \big) \big( T_1 - T \big)}} \right] \).

Hence, \( \big[ \mathbb{E}^{**}\big[ V_T \big] = \! \mathbb{E}^{*}\big[ V_T \big] +\! A \big]\), \( \big( A =\! \rho_{W,V} \sigma_V \sigma_W \big), \rho_{W,V} = -\!\rho_{R,V} \).
     \( \big[\sigma_W\big] \) can be calculated from \( \big[\sigma_R\big] \) using Ito's Lemma.
Adjustment - Quantos
\( dS_{Y \to X} = \big( r_Y - r_X \big) S_{Y \to X} dt + \sigma S_{Y \to X} dW_t \)

\( d \left( {\displaystyle\frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}}} \right) = \big( r_X - r_Y + \sigma^2 \big) \left( {\displaystyle\frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}}} \right) dt + \sigma \left( {\displaystyle\frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}}} \right) dW_t \)

Above Process is w.r.t. the Numeraire w.r.t. Money Market for Country \( \big[Y\big] \). Ideally, We need to move to Process is w.r.t. the Numeraire w.r.t. Money Market for Country \( \big[X\big] \).

So We use, the Numeraire Ratio as \( \left[ W =\! \left[ {\displaystyle\frac{g_X}{g_Y}} \right] S_{Y \to X} \right] \), \( \big[ \: \!g \: \big] \) is Money-Market Account.

So \( \big[ \mathbb{DRIFT}\big]\) will increase by \( \left[ \big( \rho_{W,V} \sigma_V \sigma_W \big), V =\! \left[{\displaystyle\frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}}} \right] \right] =\!\! \!-\!\!\! \sigma^2, \:\: \big[ \rho_{W,V} =\! -\!1, \sigma_W =\! \sigma \big]\).

Hence, \( \left[ d \left( {\displaystyle\frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}}} \right) = \big( r_X - r_Y \big) \left( {\displaystyle\frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}}} \right) dt + \sigma \left( {\displaystyle\frac{1}{S_{Y \to X}}} \right) dW_t \right] \).
