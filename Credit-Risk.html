Using Corporate Bond's Spread - A MORE EXACT Calculation [546]
\( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) a regular coupon paying Corporate Bond with Yield \( \big[y\%\big] \) & RFR \( \big[r\%\big] \) & Life \( \big[T\big] \) years.
\( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) during the life-time, this Bond can Default at times \( \big[ T_1, T_2, ..., T_n \big] \) which, usually coincides with the Coupon paying dates.
\( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) \( \left[Q =\! \mathbb{PD}_{\mathbb{UNCOND}}/\mathbb{YEAR}_{ \big[0,\: T\big]} \right] \).

So, \( \big[ \mathbb{LGD}_{T_{i}} =\! \big( B_{r,\: T_i} - R \big) Q \big] \). Here \( \big[ B_{r,\: T_i} \big] \) is the Bond's Value if NO Default at that Time i.e. When, Bond's Value is calculated using Forward RFR.

So, the PV of above is \( \big[ \big( B_{r,\: T_i} - R \big) Q \big] e^{-r T_i} \). Hence Overall PV is \( \big[ \mathbb{LGD}_{\mathbb{PV}} =\! \sum_{i=1}^{n} \big( B_{r,\: T_i} - R \big) Q e^{-r T_i} \big] \).

So, \( \big[ \big( B_{r,\: T_0} - B_{y,\: T_0} \big) =\! \mathbb{LGD}_{\mathbb{PV}}\big] \). Use this, to calculate \( \big[ Q =\! Q_{\mathbb{RISK\:NEUTRAL}} \big] \) As, We discounted using \( \big[r\big] \).
So, if \( \big[ B_{r,\: T_0} - B_{y,\: T_0} \big] \) is HIGH then, \( \big[Q\big] \) will be HIGH (this happened during 2007 Crisis).
Different Bonds will have different Maturities. Therefore, We can have Default Prob's Term-Structure. \( \mathbb{ASSUME} \). We have Bonds with Maturities \( \big[3\mathbb{Y}, 5\mathbb{Y}, 7\mathbb{Y}\big] \). 1st is used to estimate Yearly \( \mathbb{PD} \) for \( \big[ 3\mathbb{Y} \big] \). 2nd is used to estimate Yearly \( \mathbb{PD} \) for \( \big[ 4,5\mathbb{Y} \big] \) etc.
Here, the \( \mathbb{ASSUMPTION} \) is : Corporate Bond's Value only depends on \( \mathbb{PD} \). This is \( \mathbb{FALSE} \) As, it also depends on Liquidity.

Adjust : Derivative's Value for Counterparty PD i.e. cVA
\( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) I have a Derivative that lasts till time \( \big[ T \big] \). It's Default can happen at time \( \big[ T_1, T_2, ..., T_n=T \big] \).
        If, there is NO Default then, this Derivative's Value is \( \big[ f_{T_i} \big] \).
        Here, My Exposure at time \( \big[ T_i \big] \) is equal to, the Potential Loss : \( \mathbb{MAX} \big[ f_{T_i}, 0 \big] \).

So, \( \big[ \mathbb{LGD}_{\mathbb{RISK\:NEUTRAL}} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} q_{T_i} \big( 1-R \big) \mathbb{E} \big[ \mathbb{MAX} \big[ f_{T_i}, 0 \big] \big] e^{-\!r_{T_i}} \big] \), \( \big[ q_{T_i} = \mathbb{PD}_{\mathbb{RISK\:NEUTRAL}, \: T_i} \big] \). This Value to be deducted from the Derivative's Value with NO Default. Hence the cVA.

\( \big[ q_{T_i} \big] \) is is INDEP. of the Derivative's Value.

\( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) My Derivative's ONLY Payoff is at time \( \big[ T \big] \) then, the above cVA will be \( \big[ f_{T_0} \sum_{i=1}^{n} q_{T_i} \big( 1-R \big) \big] \). It is EASY to understand that. So, the Derivative's Adjusted Value will be \( \big[ f^{*}_{T_0} = f_{T_0} - f_{T_0} \sum_{i=1}^{n} q_{T_i} \big( 1-R \big) \big] \).

One such Derivative is ZCB, So \( \big[ B^{*}_{T_0} = B_{T_0} - B_{T_0} \sum_{i=1}^{n} q_{T_i} \big( 1-R \big) \big] \). Hence We have \( \left[ \frac{f^{*}_{T_0}}{B^{*}_{T_0}} \right] =\! \left[ \frac{f_{T_0}}{B_{T_0}} \right] \).
This gives \( \big[ f^{*}_{T_0} = f_{T_0} e^{- \big( y^{*} - y \big) T } \big] \). Here, \( \big[ y^{*}, y \big] \) are the Yields for My Counterparty's ZCB and Risk-Free ZCB.

This shows that: Any European style Derivative Credit-Adjusted Value can be calculated by -
Increase the Discounting Rate appropriately.

Gaussian Copula Model : The Time-To-Default
With Gaussian Copula, We estimate : The underlying Variables' CORR Structure from their Marginal Distn.
With Gaussian Copula, We represent : The CORR Structure of 2 Variables Which are NOT Normal.

\( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) there are 2 Companies & Time-To-Default are \( \big[ t_1, t_2 \big] \). Then We transform them as \( \big[ x_i = \Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( t_i \big) \big] \sim \mathbb{N} \big(0, 0, 1, 1, \rho \big), i = 1,2 \big] \) Where, \( \big[ Q_i \big] \) is \( \mathbb{CDF} \) of \( \big[ t_i \big] \). The Default \( \mathbb{CORR} \) between \( \big[ t_1, t_2 \big] \) is estimated as \( \big[ \rho \big] \) Which, is estimated as the \( \mathbb{CORR} \) between Equities.

Typically, We consider One Factor Model i.e. \( \big[ x_i = a_i F + \big( 1 - a_i^2 \big)^{0.50} Z_i \big] \). Here, \( \big[ F \sim \mathbb{N} \big(0, 1 \big) \big] \) is Common Factor affecting Companies. And, \( \big[ Z_i \big] \) affecting ith Company.

\( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) : Default will happen by time \( \big[ T \big] \) if \( \big[ x_i < \Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) \big] \). So, We have :
\( \big[ \Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) > a_i F + \big( 1 - a_i^2 \big)^{0.50} Z_i \big] \Rightarrow \left( Z_i <\! \left[ \frac{\Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) - a_i F}{\big(1 - a_i^2 \big)^{0.50}} \right] \right) \).

So, the Prob. of Default GIVEN \( \big[ F \big] \) is : \( P \left[ Z_i <\! \left[ \frac{\Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) - a_i F}{\big(1 - a_i^2 \big)^{0.50}} \right] \right] =\! \Phi \left[ \frac{\Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) - a_i F}{\big(1 - a_i^2 \big)^{0.50}} \right] \).
Credit Risk VaR - HULL
\( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) a Bank has Large Portfolio of Loans. Prob. & CORR of Defaults are ALL Same. It is TRUE that, Approximately % of Bonds will default in time \( \big[ T \big] \) is \( \left( \Phi \left[ \frac{\Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) - a_i F}{\big(1 - a_i^2 \big)^{0.50}} \right], \: a_i^2 =\! \rho \right) \).

We know that \( \big[ F \sim \mathbb{N} \big(0, 1 \big) \big] \). So, We will have :

\(x = P \big[ F > \Phi^{-1} \big(1 - x\big) \big] = P \big[ F > -\Phi^{-1} \big(x\big) \big] \)
                                                      \(= P \big[ -\rho^{0.5} F < \rho^{0.5} \Phi^{-1} \big(x\big) \big ] \)
                                                      \(= P \left[ \Phi \left[ \frac{\Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) - \rho^{0.5} F}{\big(1 - \rho \big)^{0.50}} \right] < \Phi \left[ \frac{\Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) - \rho^{0.5} \Phi^{-1} \big(x\big)}{\big(1 - \rho \big)^{0.50}} \right] \right] \)
                                                      \(= P \left[ \Phi \left[ \frac{\Phi^{-1} \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) - \rho^{0.5} F}{\big(1 - \rho \big)^{0.50}} \right] < V \big( T, x \big) \right] \)

So, there is \( \big[ x\% \big] \) Prob. that, % of Defaults in time \( \big[ T \big] \) will be LESS than \( \big[ V \big( T, x \big) \big] \). Remember, \( \big[ Q_i \big( T \big) \big] \) is the PD in time \( \big[ T \big] \).

So If, the TOTAL Loan Port. is \( \big[ $L \big] \) then, the MAX Possible Loss will be \( \big[ $L \big(1-R\big) V \big( T, x \big) \big] \). This is Credit VaR with appropriate C.F. And, the ith Bond's Contribution is \( \big[ $L_i \big(1-R\big) V \big( T, x \big) \big] \).
Credit Derivative : CDS [570]
CDS provides Insurance against Reference-Entity's Default. CDS's Buyer have RIGHT to sell Underlying Bond to the CDS's Seller @FV if, Default happens. In Return, CDS's Buyer will make Periodic Payment to CDS's Seller. Payments are made in Arrears. CDS Spread is that Periodic Payment & Annualised & expressed As, \( \big[ \% \big] \) of Notional.

CDS's Indices are : \( \big[ \mathbb{CDX\:NA\:IG},\: \mathbb{iTRAXX\:EUROPE} \big] \) for, US & EU with, \( 125 \) Investment-Grade Companies. \( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) that \( \big[ \big(\mathbb{CDX\:NA\:IG}\big)_{5\mathbb{Y}} \:= 65\mathbb{BP} \big] \) Which, is basically \( \big[\mathbb{AVERAGE} -\! \epsilon \big] \) of Underlying Companies' \( \mathbb{CDSS} \) [578]. Therefore, I can buy Insurance worth \( $800,000 \) for all \( 125 \) Companies for \( \big[125 \times\! $800,000 \times\! 0.0065\big] \) Yearly.

If, One Company defaults then, the \( \mathbb{CDSS} \) will reduce by \( {\left(\frac{1}{125}\right)}^{\mathbb{TH}} \) against Receipt of \( $800,000 \).

CDS-Bond Basis
\( \mathbb{ASSUME} \) I buy a Corporate Bond with Yield \( \big[y_{7\%}\big] \). To protect from Default, I ALSO buy a CDS with CDS-Spread \( \big[\mathbb{CDSS}_{2\%}\big] \) with SAME Maturity. So effectively, I bought a Risk-Free Bond with Yield \( \big[r_{5\%}\big] \).

Until this Bond's Default, I will earn \( \big[r_{5\%}\big] \). If this Bond default, I will get FV from My CDS then, I will invest that in Risk-Free Bond for remaining Maturity. Hence earn \( \big[r_{5\%}\big] \). Hence,
\( \big[ \big(y -\! \mathbb{CDSS}\big) -\! r =\! 0\big] \Rightarrow \big[ \mathbb{CDSS} -\! \big( y -\! r \big) =\! 0 \big] \). This is the CDS-Bond Basis.
