Create a Portfolio : \( \big[1\big] \) SHORT CALL Option & \( \left[ {\displaystyle\frac{\delta f}{\delta S}} \right] \) LONG Share/Underlying. So, We have the Portfolio PnL :

\( d\Pi =\! {\displaystyle\frac{\delta f}{\delta S}} dS \:- df \)
\( \:\:\:\:\:\: = {\displaystyle\frac{\delta f}{\delta S}} \big[ \mu S dt + \sigma S dW_t \big] \:- \left[ \mu S {\displaystyle\frac{\delta f}{\delta S}} + {\displaystyle\frac{\delta f}{\delta t}} + 0.5\sigma^2 S^2 {\displaystyle\frac{\delta^2 f}{\delta S^2}} \right]dt - \left[ \sigma S {\displaystyle\frac{\delta f}{\delta S}} \right]dW_t \)
\( \:\:\:\:\:\: = -\! \left[ {\displaystyle\frac{\delta f}{\delta t}} + 0.5\sigma^2 S^2 {\displaystyle\frac{\delta^2 f}{\delta S^2}} \right]dt \)
\( \:\:\:\:\:\: = r \Pi dt \)
\( \:\:\:\:\:\: = r \left( {\displaystyle\frac{\delta f}{\delta S}} S \:- f \right) dt \)

This will give the Final Result. To solve this for CALL Options price, we need to apply the Boundary condition as \( f_T = \mathbb{MAX} \big[ S_T - K, 0\big] \). Similarly for the PUT Options. Ofcourse for different kind of Derivatives i.e. for different shapes of \( f \), We will get different values.

Solution of above Differential equation will be the Price of a Tradable Security. If, for a Derivative's Price, above equation does not hold, then that Derivative can NOT be any Tradable Security. Therefore \( \left[ e^S \right] \) can NOT be a Derivative (there will be Arbitrage Opportunity).
